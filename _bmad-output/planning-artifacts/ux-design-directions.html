Layout & Hierarchy

01
Focus First — single hero card, maximum task clarity. Best for students with 1–2 active lessons.
02
Board Classic — tab filter shows column views. Familiar mental model, most structured.
03
Dashboard Home — data-first. Best for students who want to see their standing before picking work.
04
Dark Focus — luminous states on dark. Cleanest at night, state colors most visible.
05
Accordion — In Progress always front. Collapsed sections reduce visual overwhelm for long backlogs.
06
Today's Plan — session-framed. Golden Yellow hero sets the emotional tone for each study session.

Emotional Feel

01
Calm clarity. Blue header grounds the session, yellow accent pulls attention to live work.
02
Structured and professional. Most "task manager" — least emotional warmth.
03
Confident and data-anchored. Coverage ring = honest progress. Honest confidence energy.
04
Focused and calm. Dark mode reduces stimulation. Good for deep study sessions.
05
Manageable. Collapsed sections make the backlog feel less overwhelming — good for anxious students.
06
Most motivating. "Today's Plan" framing makes each session feel intentional and bounded.

Questions to Consider

→
Which direction would Kavya (first week) find least overwhelming on first open?
→
Which direction makes the Re-opened (Dusty Rose) state most visible at a glance?
→
Does dark mode feel right as the default, or should a light direction be the entry experience?
→
Should the dashboard data (coverage %) be always visible, or discovered via navigation?
→
Can elements from multiple directions be combined? (e.g., D01 layout with D04 dark mode)

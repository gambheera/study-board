# Product Brief: StudyBoard

## Executive Summary

Sri Lanka produces roughly 300,000 Advanced Level candidates each year. These students have more content than they can consume — YouTube tutorials, tuition classes, textbooks, past paper archives. The bottleneck is not content. It is execution. StudyBoard is a mobile-first study operating system that turns the A/L exam into a managed Agile project — giving students a structured backlog, enforced quality gates, and real-time progress visibility for the first time.

Version 1 targets A/L Chemistry students. StudyBoard converts the entire syllabus into an Agile-style task board: Backlog → To-Do → In Progress → Done. "Done" is not self-reported. A student cannot mark a lesson complete without passing its comprehensive quiz. Fail the quiz, and the task re-opens. The student cannot lie to themselves about their progress — and that trust mechanic is what makes every number on the dashboard honest.

Before studying a lesson, students encounter past paper questions from that topic — creating curiosity, priming the mind, and dramatically improving absorption. Learning driven by curiosity is a universal principle: knowledge that answers a question the mind has already asked is retained far more deeply than knowledge delivered unprompted. Combined with a dopamine-driven mobile experience where every tap is designed to feel rewarding rather than stressful, StudyBoard turns disciplined study into a habit students want to keep.

## The Problem

A/L students in Sri Lanka face three compounding failures that no existing platform addresses.

**No execution system.** The A/L syllabus is finite and well-defined, yet students treat it as a shapeless mountain — studying whatever feels urgent, skipping difficult topics, arriving at exam time with unknown coverage gaps. Nobody has given them a system to turn that mountain into a completable plan.

**Disconnected past paper practice.** Past papers are the most effective preparation tool available, but students attempt them year by year with no link to their syllabus coverage. They cannot tell which topics they have practiced thoroughly, which they have never touched, or what their accuracy rate is per topic. Real practice generates no real intelligence.

**No measurable progress.** Without visibility, students study from fear and reactivity — not intention and confidence. As the exam approaches, this uncertainty becomes paralyzing.

Beneath all three is a deeper problem: students who cannot see their progress cannot take ownership of their preparation. They study because they are anxious, not because they have a plan.

## The Solution

StudyBoard runs the A/L exam like an Agile software project. The student is both the product owner and the developer. The syllabus is the backlog.

Every lesson, sub-lesson, past paper question, and predicted future paper question becomes a task. Students work across three content tracks — Theory, Past Papers, and Future Papers — moving tasks through Backlog → To-Do → In Progress → Done.

**Definition of Done is enforced.** A lesson is only complete when the student passes its comprehensive quiz. Fail the quiz, the task re-opens. This single mechanism transforms the dashboard from a vanity metric into a trustworthy signal. Progress is real because the system verifies it.

**Curiosity precedes instruction.** Before studying a lesson, students are exposed to past paper questions from that topic. The questions create the "why should I understand this?" before the lesson answers it. This is not a design philosophy — it is how learning works. Questions before answers. Curiosity before content.

**The experience is dopamine-driven.** Completing a task triggers a visual reward. Finishing a topic unlocks the next. Streaks build momentum. The app removes the stress of study and replaces it with the satisfaction of forward progress. Every tap, transition, and animation is intentional. The experience should feel as rewarding as it is useful.

A real-time progress dashboard gives students an honest view of where they stand: syllabus coverage percentage, past paper completion rate, correct rate per topic, and a ranked list of their weakest areas. Any student can open the app and know exactly what to do next.

The offline-first architecture is not a technical footnote — it is a genuine differentiator in a market where many students study in areas with poor connectivity. StudyBoard works everywhere, every time.

## What Makes This Different

Every EdTech platform in the Sri Lankan A/L space is a content repository. Watch this video. Download this paper. The implicit assumption is that content access is the bottleneck. It is not.

StudyBoard makes four bets no competitor in this market has made:

1. **Execution over content** — the bottleneck is the system, not the material
2. **Enforced quality gates** — Done means proven, not claimed
3. **Curiosity-first pedagogy** — questions before lessons, not lessons before questions
4. **Dopamine-driven UX** — the experience itself is the engagement mechanism

The A/L syllabus is finite. StudyBoard converts that finite body of work into a completable, measurable, satisfying project. That is a fundamentally different product category from anything that currently exists in this market.

## Who This Serves

**Primary: A/L Chemistry students, aged 16–19.** They attend tuition classes on top of school. They study in evenings and weekends. University placement, family investment, and career trajectory all depend on this one exam. They are Android-native and mobile-first. Many study in areas with unreliable mobile data. They are not casual learners — they are students under real pressure looking for every legitimate edge they can find.

**Future users (architecturally considered in V1):** Parents who want transparent progress visibility. Tuition class instructors — the most trusted figures in a student's A/L journey — who represent both a distribution channel and a future cohort management product.

## Success Criteria

V1 success is measured against the test group:

- Measurable improvement in test scores for students who use the system consistently
- Students report studying with reduced anxiety and greater clarity about what to do next
- Consistent daily engagement — students return to the board and advance their tasks
- Complete syllabus coverage achieved before exam day — no topics left untouched
- The model is validated: structured execution produces better outcomes than unstructured study

## Scope

**In V1:**
- A/L Chemistry syllabus fully mapped as a task board
- Three content tracks: Theory, Past Papers, Future Papers (infrastructure reserved; data populated via separate workflow)
- Agile board with Backlog → To-Do → In Progress → Done workflow
- Enforced Definition of Done via comprehensive quiz per lesson (manual content)
- Curiosity-first flow: past paper question exposure before lesson study
- Real-time progress dashboard (coverage %, accuracy per topic, weakest topics ranked)
- Rich media support: chemical formulas, molecular diagrams, images in questions and lesson notes
- Offline-first, Android-first
- Free during test and validation period

**Explicitly out of V1:**
- Other A/L subjects (Physics, Biology, Economics, etc.)
- Parent-facing and teacher-facing features
- Tiered premium plans and daily standup coaching
- In-app content marketplace
- AI-powered study recommendations

## Vision

StudyBoard begins with one subject and one exam system. The model is universal.

The same architecture — syllabus as backlog, enforced quality gates, curiosity-first pedagogy, dopamine-driven UX — applies to every A/L subject, every O/L subject, and every professional certification examination. Sri Lanka is the proof of concept. The platform is built from day one to scale beyond it.

The daily standup plan — where a scrum master-style coach facilitates daily progress reviews for small student cohorts — represents a premium service that could redefine how tuition classes operate. The in-app content marketplace creates a content economy where validated lesson materials become an additional revenue stream alongside tiered subscriptions.

The first version of StudyBoard proves the model. If it demonstrably improves Chemistry exam scores for a small test group, the path to becoming the study operating system for an entire generation of students is open. Not just a better study app — a new product category that treats every examination as a managed project and every student as a capable, data-driven professional of their own learning.
